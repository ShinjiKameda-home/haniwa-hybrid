# Smart HANIWA: Timeless yet Cutting-edge Irrigation and Scarecrow Hybrid System

## Motivation: Bridging the Past and Future – A New Approach to Japanese Irrigation

Did you know which country holds the title for the world's oldest continuous monarchy? It’s Japan. While our schools don't often dwell on this sense of continuity, I find a quiet pride in the history of the Kofun—the ancient imperial mounds.

There’s a fascinating theory that the Haniwa (terracotta clay figures) placed on these mounds weren't just funeral offerings. Positioned along coastlines, these mounds may have acted as beacons, with Haniwas' hollow bodies flickering with light to guide seafarers home. It suggests a kind ruler who wished to protect their people even in their afterlife.

I recently brought home a Haniwa replica. It's more than a souvenir　for me — it seems the perfect vessel for Terracotta Irrigation.

By utilizing the natural porosity of the clay, I’m transforming this ancient figure into a functional Olla irrigation system for my small garden. Combined with a Raspberry Pi Pico W, this 'Smart Haniwa' will monitor soil moisture and manage water levels, bridging 1,500 years of history with modern IoT.

My Vision: The Haniwa Guardian of the Potager
I’m transforming this ancient guardian into a hybrid "Smart Scarecrow" and "Terracotta Irrigation System" for my small garden.

 - The "Terracotta Irrigation": Utilizing the porous nature of clay for automatic, low-tech irrigation.

 - Predictive Intelligence: Integrating a Raspberry Pi Pico W with weather data and soil sensors. It won't just water the plants; it’ll notify me via Telegram when it needs a refill.

 - Active Defense: In my previous project, I hacked home security camera feeds for "Bird Watching", it will be usable to trigger glowing LED on Haniwa's eyes and a buzzer to keep crows away.

In Japan’s limited space, there is a distinct beauty in multipurpose design. Let’s breathe new life into an ancient form.
